### Volunteering: translating Twitter into Russian

I have been interested in Twitter since 2011, although lately I no longer use it as much. ![Sharing your ideas on Twitter is really easy](https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/pw/ABLVV84Gw8EcF_RFKmSEUbKdcJ9sR0K9lj6SPQMnkHWu5Xl5KYviz11q5lsSfdqzuljaI_ZpA3VJWCqnCpPH1svWbOu41zjTm8-tVKwVCtGMKERIE5Vn=w2400){: .shadow }
I would like to share my experience with this service. I believe Twitter still remains one of the best platforms for expressing your opinion and your position.

One of the most memorable experiences for me was taking part in translating **Twitter** into Russian — all the more so because in my native language there were no such words as _"tweet"_, _"retweet"_ and _"twitter"_. Many other terms, such as the meaning of _"follow"_, would also carry a different meaning — or would it be more appropriate to say _"suivre"_? (and yes, we are going to talk about how we translated Twitter from English into Russian).



It is important to start with a crucial point: for me this activity was not a job, and I never thought I would one day describe this experience as "volunteering". As a student devoting his free time to translations and an active Twitter user, I was motivated by the desire to earn the translator badge.![Twitter Translate Badge](https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/pw/ABLVV84cL13wrD-Td6Tv2r1RqUW_MZTQKN5wu1Wj7qoG6uaOMLkOhnlaavbl9KmrDQK7SYbU2otWMTS2tUSC6JwqDFQDz0Ae26brDIiNjsUJH-Lr77CF=w2400){: .shadow : .left }

By clicking on this 'Twitter Translate Badge' icon, you could see the translations proposed by the **Twitter** user and confirmed. However, it was not that simple. To earn the badge you had to not only vote actively for translations, but also have confirmed translations of your own. Nobody knew how many translations were needed to get the badge, and many of those who started _"working"_ for it quickly gave up. Only after several months did the long-awaited change in my profile finally catch my eye, along with the "planet" reward — the blue planet badge leading to my translator profile. Even after receiving this reward, I kept actively voting on translations and proposing my own corrections.

![Machine translation](https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/pw/ABLVV87wKoWagYl3KGutmPkKtSjJX65irWn380YcVjpyMPwfrjKTiUlftRyR2yIDqNV6gY1m9RogkFG0GOErrxMS-aW7n6qr9cOEDhhimqZ_uOHdMcGW=w2400){: .shadow }{: .right }



In particular, by removing unverified _"machine translation"_. I understand that I make mistakes too, but on the "personal field" you have to learn from your own mistakes. ![Machine translation on Twitter](https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/pw/ABLVV87U66cHIQ7aZdUA3dn8FBTQN8jv2leKBHCRBPyiBqzfUnDophFC3hbAaBpMFKFgqeV83TXV30po9I8bTPtCADOauFrUEtDJ4f20vr73tNRfj5WL=w2400){: .shadow }
However, when translating world news, it is essential to put an end to amateurism and treat it as real work!

### End of the service

Since **November 1, 2017**, the Twitter Translation Center has ceased operations.

![Twitter Translation Center are closed](https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/pw/ABLVV85UjzUByq2ijifag0BCU44-0n7VVQfHvhACeLpIoYKjyQ2LjfytaKpyiED3sDoTZ6uck7kZLcIMfJP3sK6RtPM8aTKyXJJwsvZIjWF_cUfFPmVz=w2400){: .shadow }

Perhaps Elon Musk's team will relaunch this volunteer translation service one day, but for now all the profiles and statistics are inaccessible. All that remains, as a keepsake for the community's most active participants, are the _"badges"_. Their origin and authenticity can be confirmed in the page's source code. ![Twitter source code](https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/pw/ABLVV86ZjDA7ZQyKYZuQuhpIxQd17pwhZRaE7VOI6We1qmol3n5U92ZRQ9pQNZIfKTIu5khQYH0sw51FjGBZKXJ_PprhKqI_vMH4oSD4mPotTOq9Ymi9=w2400){: .shadow }
